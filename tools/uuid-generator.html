What is a UUID?

A UUID (Universally Unique Identifier) is a 128-bit number used to identify information in computer systems. The probability of generating duplicate UUIDs is extremely low, making them ideal for distributed systems.

Format: xxxxxxxx-xxxx-4xxx-yxxx-xxxxxxxxxxxx
